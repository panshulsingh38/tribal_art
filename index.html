Tribal Art & Tradition Showcase

Exploring the Rich Cultural Landscapes of Telangana and Jharkhand

Telangana

Cheriyal Scroll Painting

A stylized narrative art form crafted in a distinct comic-strip format. Using vibrant natural dyes laid over a rich, signature bright red base, these historic scrolls beautifully depict ancient Indian folklore, regional legends, and village life.

Jharkhand

Sohrai & Khovar Murals

Traditional ritualistic wall paintings passed down through generations. Created by tribal women using natural soils, Sohrai honors harvest abundance and livestock, while Khovar introduces striking geometric canvas work inside bridal chambers.

Telangana Exhibit

Cheriyal Narrative Scrolls

Cheriyal scroll painting is an exquisite, highly localized version of the Nakashi art form, preserved almost exclusively in Telangana. These paintings are structured explicitly like modern graphic novels or comic strips, running vertically as long scrolls to tell step-by-step community history and legendary folktales.

🎨 Raw Mediums

Crafted entirely using natural mineral extracts, leaves, and tree gums. The bright red backdrop is the historical signature of a true Cheriyal piece.

📜 Storytelling

Historically accompanied by wandering balladeers who sang the folk stories out loud while unrolling the canvas in rural public squares.

Jharkhand Exhibit

Sohrai & Khovar Murals

Sohrai and Khovar are organic wall arts native to the Hazaribagh region of Jharkhand. Traditionally executed on mud walls during changing agricultural and matrimonial seasons, these forms capture a deep spiritual reverence for wildlife, domestic animals, and tribal lineage.

🌾 Sohrai Harvest

Painted right after the monsoons to celebrate bountiful crop yields and thank domestic livestock. Features bold silhouettes of forest animals and birds.

💍 Khovar Nuptials

Created inside marriage chambers by the bride's family. Uses a distinct scratch technique to uncover dark soil layers beneath white clay coats, revealing beautiful patterns.
